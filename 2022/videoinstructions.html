Video Presentation Instructions – SISAP 2021

Authors of regular and short papers have the following duties for the conference:

  1. They must pre-record a presentation of the work, no longer than 25 minutes in duration. You are encouraged to convey the main ideas in a shorter time. This is in particular recommended for short papers.

  2. The authors must upload a signed Video/Audio Presentation License to allow distributing the recording. If this is not possible for you, please let us know and propose an alternative solution.

  3. They must give a 5-minute summary during the live Zoom session and subsequently answer questions from the audience for up to 5 minutes.

  4. They should create a simple poster for the online poster room.

  5. They must attend two of the three poster sessions scattered through the conference programme (suitable for different time zones).

Please record the videos with a software of your choice, and store them in mp4 format. It is recommended to have large slides with large fonts, but also to include a video of the presenter. A popular free choice is OpenBroadcastStudio (OBS) for Windows, OSX, and Linux, a popular commercial choice is Camtasia. Apparently, some people even like recording in an empty Zoom room.

The videos will be made accessible via a YouTube channel for the conference, and above license is necessary such that we can upload your presentation. Hence you must not re-use copyrighted material from others in your presentation without a license.

For examples, see last year’s SISAP 2020 channel: https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCHGbhzq1cqOr-ta01uAXVZg/videos

The video must be uploaded before September 22, to ensure we can make it available on-time. Please do not wait until the last day.

Video upload

  1. The video must be in mp4 format
  2. The file should be named xx_yyyyy.mp4, where xx is your submission ID, and yyyyy is replaced with the last name of the first author. So if your paper id were 42, and your last name were Adams, please name the file 42_Adams.mp4.
  3. The Video/Audio Presentation License form must be signed an uploaded, please name it accordingly xx_yyyyy.pdf.
  4. Optionally, you can include a text file with a short description and related links (e.g., source code repository) that should be included in the video description.
    You can also use this file to let us know if you prefer your video to be unlisted (i.e., not included in YouTube search), although this may reduce the visibility of your research and is not recommended.
  5. Upload via Dropbox https://www.dropbox.com/request/zej2AB87EeL6i3NyOSuJ,
    Alternate upload options: https://tu-dortmund.sciebo.de/s/Ir83CukR5CLVZaj and https://owncloud.cs.tu-dortmund.de/index.php/s/tPikslWBZO1cPlV (the password was in the email).
    In case neither works for you, you can also upload the video to your own cloud storage or web server, and send an email to erich.schubert@tu-dortmund.de with the links to these files.

If we cannot get the video to play, we may ask you to provide another version.

Poster upload

Posters will be presented in two out of three sessions (to your choice, to accomodate different time zones).

Posters will be shown in half a screen width. They will be scrollable. Hence a vertical layout works best:

You can upload the following formats along with your videos:

  • Recommended: a Markdown file. We will enable MathJax for formulae. If you use images, please prefix them with your name.
  • a PNG image
  • a single PDF file (we may need to convert this to PNG to improve the user experience)

Until your poster contents are integrated, we use the paper title and abstract in the posters list. Here is an example Markdown poster with MathJax equations.
