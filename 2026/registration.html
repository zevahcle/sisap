Instructions for Registering Authors

When registering as an author, you will be asked to provide the paper ID of your submission as shown in the EasyChair system. If you do not have a paper in the conference, simply indicate that the paper ID does not apply to your registration.

Please note the following policy for accepted papers regarding registration:

  • Each accepted paper must have at least one author with a full registration by August TBD?, 2026.
    Student registration is not valid for this purpose, unless the author is a PhD student presenting only a Doctoral Symposium paper. In that case, the student registration fee applies.
  • A full registration of an accepted SISAP paper can cover one additional paper, provided that:
    • (a) the additional paper is of type Doctoral Symposium, Demonstration, or Indexing Challenge, and
    • (b) the same registered author is also the presenting author of both papers.
  • A single registration cannot cover two regular or special session papers. In that case, each paper must have its own separate full registration..

Country Entrance and Visa

The Czech Republic and all its four neighbouring countries are members of the EU and of the Schengen Area. Citizens of all EU Member States may enter the Czech Republic using a valid national identity card or a valid passport. There are no routine border checks for travellers at the Czech Republic's land borders with neighbouring states. Still, random checks of travel documents may be carried out, with a greater focus on international transport.

Non-EU citizens travelling to the Schengen Area for short stays (up to 90 days) are required to use the European Entry/Exit System (EES). The system registers non-EU nationals who require visas as well as those who travel visa-free. See the link for details and lists of countries.
