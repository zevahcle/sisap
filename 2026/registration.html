Registration

Registration provides access to all three days of the conference, including lunches, coffee breaks, and the conference banquet. Registrations will be managed by the Masaryk University. Please read the page carefully before registering.


The Czech Republic does not use the Euro. The fees will be charged in Czech koruna (CZK). Payments are made via online credit card payments. Accepted cards are VISA and Mastercard. If you are unable to complete registration in this way, please contact: sisap2026@fi.muni.cz

In case of issues related to registration, please contact us at: sisap2026@fi.muni.cz

Instructions for Registering Authors

  • Each accepted paper must be covered by either a Type A or a Type B registration by August 13, 2026.
  • Every conference participant who presents a paper (of any type, i.e., regular long, regular short, student, demo, position, or Indexing Challenge solution) must have at least one Type A registration.
  • If a conference participant delivers n > 1 oral presentations at the conference in person, (s)he may use Type B registrations to cover n - 1 papers (s)he co-authored and presents.
  • Type C registration is intended for conference participants with no oral presentation of any type, although they may (and don't have to) be co-authors of an accepted paper.
Therefore, most of the papers will have to be covered by the standard registration of Type A. If two authors of the same (single) paper decide to visit the conference, the presenting author has to pay Type A registration, while the second author pays only Type C registration.

Country Entrance and Visa

The Czech Republic and all its four neighbouring countries are members of the EU and of the Schengen Area. Citizens of all EU Member States may enter the Czech Republic using a valid national identity card or a valid passport. There are no routine border checks for travellers at the Czech Republic's land borders with neighbouring states. Still, random checks of travel documents may be carried out, with a greater focus on international transport and borders.

Non-EU citizens travelling to the Schengen Area for short stays (up to 90 days) are required to use the European Entry/Exit System (EES). The system registers non-EU nationals who require visas as well as those who travel visa-free. See the link for details and lists of countries. The instruction how to apply for a Schengen Visa is here.
