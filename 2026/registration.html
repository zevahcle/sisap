Instructions for Registering Authors

  • Each accepted paper must be covered by either a Type A or a Type B registration by August 13, 2026.
  • Every conference participant who presents a paper (of any type, i.e., regular long, regular short, student, demo, position, or Indexing Challenge solution) must have at least one Type A registration.
  • If a conference participant delivers n > 1 oral presentations at the conference in person, (s)he may use Type B registrations to cover n - 1 papers (s)he co-authored.
  • Type C registration is intended for conference participants with no oral presentation any type, although they may (and don't have to) be co-authors of an accepted paper.

When registering as an author, you will be asked to provide the paper ID of your submission as shown in the EasyChair system. If you do not have a paper in the conference, indicate that the paper ID does not apply to your registration.

Country Entrance and Visa

The Czech Republic and all its four neighbouring countries are members of the EU and of the Schengen Area. Citizens of all EU Member States may enter the Czech Republic using a valid national identity card or a valid passport. There are no routine border checks for travellers at the Czech Republic's land borders with neighbouring states. Still, random checks of travel documents may be carried out, with a greater focus on international transport.

Non-EU citizens travelling to the Schengen Area for short stays (up to 90 days) are required to use the European Entry/Exit System (EES). The system registers non-EU nationals who require visas as well as those who travel visa-free. See the link for details and lists of countries.
