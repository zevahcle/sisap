Conference Venue

SISAP 2026 will be held at the Faculty of Informatics, Masaryk University, Brno, Czech Republic, Botanicka 68a, 602 00 Brno, Czech Republic (website, map, interactive 3D model of the interior).

To enter the building, use, e.g., the rotating doors (you have to rotate them by yourself). The actual conference will be in KYPO (Cybernetic Proving Ground). The slideshow showing the route in the building is here .


How to get to Brno

Airports

Brno is surrounded by many airports and we list only 4 most convenient.
  • Brno airport (IATA: BRQ, aerial distance 9 km from the venue).

    The city has two airports, a small one and a tiny one. The international airport offers regular flights to Milan/Bergamo (Italy), London/Stansted (UK), and Málaga (Spain). In addition, a few more routes to summer destinations (mainly in Greece, Egypt and Turkey) are still operated in October.

  • Vienna airport (IATA: VIE, aerial distance 121 km from the venue).

    The capital of Austria provides the most convenient large hub for travels to Brno. Both direct and indirect buses and trains of multiple companies operate between Vienna airport and Brno (see below).

  • Bratislava airport (IATA: BTS, aerial distance 124 km from the venue).

    The airport in the Slovak capital can be an alternative to Vienna Airport, as it also offers plenty of connections. Travelling to Brno is, however, much more complicated, and the best way usually requires a transfer at Bratislava's main bus station.

  • Prague airport (IATA: PRG, aerial distance 196 km from the venue).

    The airport in the Czech capital is located in the north-west of the city, i.e., in the opposite direction from Brno. About 6 direct busses opperate between Prague Airport and Brno per day. Passing through Prague using the local public transportation is time-consuming and can be complicated (or expensive). See below.

Inter-city trains and buses

Central Europe has extensive public transportation coverage. Many companies operate buses and trains, including international transportation. The drawback is the typical incompatibility of their systems and tickets, which can even include traps: e.g., trains denoted as RJ are "RailJet trains" operated by CzechRailways. However, RegioJet is another company, and their tickets are not valid on trains denoted as RJ. International companies usually run their own websites and mobile apps, where one can find connections and buy a ticket at once to be sure about the vehicle (and its colour) to search for.
  • From Brno airport:

    Trolleybus 21 and bus 77 operate between Brno Airport and the city center between ~5:00 and ~22:30 while night bus N89 operates between ~22:30 and ~5:00.
