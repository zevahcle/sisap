How to get to Brno

Airports

Brno is surrounded by many airports and we list only 4 most convenient.
  • Brno airport (IATA: BRQ, aerial distance 9 km from the venue).

    The city has two airports, a small one and a tiny one. The international airport offers regular flights to Milan/Bergamo (Italy), London/Stansted (UK), and Málaga (Spain). In addition, a few more routes to summer destinations (mainly in Greece, Egypt and Turkey) are still operated in October.

  • Vienna airport (IATA: VIE, aerial distance 121 km).

    The capital of Austria provides the most convenient large hub for travels to Brno. Both direct and indirect buses and trains of multiple companies operate between Vienna airport and Brno (see below).

  • Bratislava airport (IATA: BTS, aerial distance 124 km).

    The airport in the Slovak capital can be an alternative to Vienna Airport, as it also offers plenty of connections. Travelling to Brno is, however, much more complicated, and the best way usually requires a transfer at Bratislava's main bus station.

  • Prague airport (IATA: PRG, aerial distance 196 km).

    The airport in the Czech capital is located in the north-west of the city, i.e. in the opposite direction from Brno. Rather rare direct buses operate between Prague Airport and Brno. Passing through Prague using local public transport is time-consuming and can be complicated (or expensive). Note in particular that bus AE (Airport Express) in Prague is 4 times more expensive (200 CZK) than ordinary public buses and underground (50 CZK), while not significantly faster.


Inter-city trains and buses

Many companies operate buses and trains, including international transport. The drawback is the typical incompatibility of their systems and tickets, which can even include traps: Three different companies, namely Czech Railways, RegioJet and Austrian ÖBB denote their connections as RJ<NUMBER>, but in some cases, a ticket bought via one company is not valid for a connection operated by another. Among other cases, tickets bought via Czech Railways and RegioJet are never interchangeable. RegioJet usually uses lowercase rj, while Czech Railways and ÖBB use uppercase RJ. To avoid issues, please pay attention to method of ticket purchase and the colour of the vehicle. International companies usually run their own websites and mobile apps, where one can find connections and buy a ticket directly to be sure about the vehicle's colour to look for.

Note: The later you buy a ticket for an international journey, the more expensive it usually is. This applies to all carriers listed below. If multiple companies operate the same route, their connections usually interleaves. It might be good to check them all.

The most important companies are:

  • RegioJet (website + eshop, Android app, iOS app)

    Founded in Brno in 2009, it is one of Europe’s largest long-distance bus operators. It provides rail and bus transport across around 15 countries.

    • Direct busses of this company often commute between Vienna Airport and Brno. The bus stop is directly in front of Terminal ‒ busses to Brno usually (practically always) use Platform 2.
    • Rarely, direct RegioJet trains go from the Vienna airport to Brno as well, so be careful whether it is a train or a bus.
    • There are a few direct connections between Brno and Prague airport operated by RegioJet. Also their connections with a single change in the Prague city center (Florenc) are those most convenient.

    Their busses and trains are typically yellow. In case of emergency, bus can be also white.

    Alert: RegioJet trains operate, among others, the Budapest–Vienna Hbf–Brno(–Prague) route. They never operate on the Graz–Vienna Hbf–Brno(-Prague) route and their tickets are not valid on any train running there.

  • Flix Bus (website + eshop, Android app, iOS app)

    While the German company Flix is running also the trains, they operate only the buses in Czechia.

    • Direct busses of this company often commute between Vienna Airport and Brno. The bus stop is directly in front of Terminal ‒ busses to Brno usually (practically always) use Platform 2.
    • There are a few direct connections between Brno and Prague airportoperated by FlixBus. Also their connections with a single change in the Prague city center (Florenc) are those most convenient.

    Their busses and trains are typically green. If not, the bus wears a large Flix logo.

  • Czech Railways (website + eshop, Android app, iOS app)

    The national company (state-owned) operates trains only. Among other directions, they operate some of the trains to Vienna (usually Hbf only, not the airport), Prague, and Bratislava. They also operate direct trains between Prague and Copenhagen (Denmark) via Berlin, Hamburg, Kolding and Odense. Typically, the Czech Railways' vagoons are blue.

    Alert:

    A ticket from Czech Railways (e.g. Brno to Vienna Airport) may cover multiple segments, including those run by foreign operators completely in the foreign country (e.g. Austrian ÖBB between Vienna Main Train Station (Hbf) and airport). However, buying the foreign segment through Czech Railways is several times more expensive than buying it directly from the foreign operator. A single ticket bought at an ÖBB machine costs ~€4.50 for the Hbf–airport journey in Vienna.

    Trains between Vienna Main Train Station and Brno can also be operated by red trains of ÖBB Since they operate in Czechia, technically Czech Railways bring responsibility and sell tickets for these (for common prices) in their shops without distinguishing the trains.
