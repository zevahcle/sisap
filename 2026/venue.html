How to get to Brno

Airports

Brno is surrounded by many airports and we list only 4 most convenient.
  • Brno airport (IATA: BRQ, aerial distance 9 km from the venue).

    The city has two airports, a small one and a tiny one. The international airport offers regular flights to Milan/Bergamo (Italy), London/Stansted (UK), and Málaga (Spain). In addition, a few more routes to summer destinations (mainly in Greece, Egypt and Turkey) are still operated in October.

  • Vienna airport (IATA: VIE, aerial distance 121 km).

    The capital of Austria provides the most convenient large hub for travels to Brno. Both direct and indirect buses and trains of multiple companies operate between Vienna airport and Brno (see below).

  • Bratislava airport (IATA: BTS, aerial distance 124 km).

    The airport in the Slovak capital can be an alternative to Vienna Airport, as it also offers plenty of connections. Travelling to Brno is, however, much more complicated, and the best way usually requires a transfer at Bratislava's main bus station.

  • Prague airport (IATA: PRG, aerial distance 196 km).

    The airport in the Czech capital is located in the north-west of the city, i.e. in the opposite direction from Brno. About 6 direct buses operate between Prague Airport and Brno per day. Passing through Prague using local public transport is time-consuming and can be complicated (or expensive). Note in particular that bus AE (Airport Express) in Prague is 4 times more expensive (200 CZK) than ordinary public buses and underground (50 CZK), while not significantly faster.


Inter-city trains and buses

Many companies operate buses and trains, including international transport. The drawback is the typical incompatibility of their systems and tickets, which can even include traps: e.g., trains RJ<NUMBER> are "RailJet trains" operated by Czech Railways. However, RegioJet is a different company, and their tickets are not valid on trains denoted as RJ<number>. International companies usually run their own websites and mobile apps, where one can find connections and buy a ticket directly to be sure about the vehicle (and its colour) to search for.

Note: The later you buy a ticket for an international journey, the more expensive it usually is. This applies to all carriers listed below. If multiple companies operate the same route, their connections usually interleaves. It might be good to check them all.

The most important companies are:

  • RegioJet (website + eshop, Android app, iOS app)

    It is one of Europe’s largest long-distance bus operators. Founded in Brno in 2009, the company remains under the ownership of its original founder, a single Czech businessman. Today, it provides international rail and bus transport across around 15 European countries. Their busses and trains are typically yellow. In case of emergency, bus can be also white. All the direct connections between Brno and Prague airport are those operated by RegioJet. Also their connections with a single change in the Prague city center (Florenc) are those most convenient.

    Alert: Trains RailJet operating between Graz-Vienna Hbf-Brno

  • Czech Railways (website + eshop, Android app, iOS app)

    The national company (state-owned) operates trains only. Typically, it uses blue colour. Among other directions, they operate some of the trains to Vienna, Prague, and Bratislava. They also operate direct trains between Prague and Copenhagen (Denmark) via Berlin, Hamburg and Kolding.

    Alert: A single ticket purchased via Czech Railways (e.g., from Brno to Vienna Airport) may cover multiple travel segments, some of which are operated by a foreign carrier (e.g., Austrian ÖBB operating the segment between Vienna Main Station (Hbf) and the airport). In such cases, buying the foreign segment through Czech Railways is several times more expensive than purchasing it directly from the foreign operator. A single tickets bought physically in the machine of ÖBB costs ~4.5 € per a single journey between the airport and Hbf.
